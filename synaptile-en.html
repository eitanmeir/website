The concept

What is SynapTile™?

SynapTile is a hands-on educational hardware system that lets people build and understand simple neural networks physically, using modular electronic tiles.

At its core, SynapTile turns the idea of a perceptron into a physical object. Each tile represents one simple decision-making unit: it receives several inputs, combines them, applies a threshold-like behavior, and produces an output.

When multiple tiles are connected together, they can form a larger network that behaves more like a real neural system.

How a tile works

Each SynapTile tile behaves like a simplified neuron or perceptron. It receives signals, processes them, and decides whether to activate.

  • 3 excitatory inputs - signals that push the tile toward activation
  • 1 inhibitory input - a signal that suppresses or blocks activation
  • Threshold behavior - reacts only when enough signal is present
  • 1 output - feeds the result to the next tile in the chain
  • Visual feedback - LEDs or light show the signal flow

What makes it special: SynapTile uses light as part of the decision-making mechanism. Learners can literally see when a signal becomes strong, when a node activates, and how signals propagate through a chain.

Excitation and inhibition

Intelligent behavior is not only about adding signals. It is also about blocking, gating, and suppressing them.

  • Excitatory signals increase the probability of activation
  • Inhibitory signals suppress the output - even when excitation is present
  • Demonstrates competition, prevention, conditional activation, and control logic

This teaches a crucial principle of neuroscience and AI: intelligence emerges from the interplay of excitation and inhibition.

From one tile to a network

A single tile shows the basic principle. The real power of SynapTile appears when multiple tiles are connected together.

A layered decision system can emerge:

  • 1. First layer detects simple conditions (light, touch, safety)
  • 2. Second layer combines those conditions
  • 3. Final tile represents the overall decision
  • 4. An inhibitory tile blocks the result if a danger condition exists

The result: "Activate only if several good conditions are true, unless a blocking condition is present."

That is already very close to real decision logic found in both biology and AI.

A simple analogy

Imagine a tiny organism deciding whether to move toward food.

It receives several signals: "there is light," "there is warmth," "there is food smell" - but also "there is danger."

If enough positive signals are present, the organism acts. If the danger signal is strong, the action is blocked.

That is exactly the kind of behavior SynapTile can demonstrate.

Each tile becomes a tiny decision unit in that creature's nervous system. When several tiles are connected together, the learner can build a physical model of how simple intelligence can emerge from many small decisions.
