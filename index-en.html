Balance

Number Harmony Game. Learn math through play.

An engaging mobile game that builds mathematical thinking through interactive number-balance challenges. Ideal from age 9 for problem-solving and number sense.

Ages 9+ iPhone & iPad $1.99

What is Balance

Balance is a learning game for mobile devices that builds mathematical thinking through interactive number-harmony challenges. Players balance left and right values through smart number choices and train problem-solving, number sense and mathematical thinking in a playful, engaging way.

Number harmony

Master the art of balancing numbers. Players learn to compute differences and choose numbers strategically to keep left and right in balance.

Strategic thinking

Develop critical thinking through forward-looking moves. Every decision affects the balance and trains strategic number choice and placement.

Real-time feedback

See the difference between left and right instantly. Visual feedback helps understand mathematical relationships and learn from every move.

Points & progress

Track progress with points and move count. Score more with fewer moves. Train efficient problem-solving.

Rising difficulty

Thoughtful levels with increasing complexity. Each level builds on the previous. Challenging but achievable.

Ideal from age 9

Designed for children and learners from age 9. No prior knowledge needed. Great for beginners and building confident number sense.

JuniorCoder

For aspiring software developers

A learning app for iPad that teaches children the basics of programming. Your child learns loops, steps to solve puzzles and the right, efficient path through the levels.

Ages 4+ iPad $1.99

What is JuniorCoder

JuniorCoder is a learning game that teaches children core programming concepts through interactive, visual programming. Children chain movement commands and guide a robot to the star. Along the way they learn how programming works in a playful way.

Visual programming

Complex programming concepts simplified through visual representation. Children see their programs as clear step sequences. Abstract ideas become tangible.

Sequential thinking

Learn that programs execute commands one after another. Children understand that order matters and each command has a specific effect.

Step planning

Break problems into smaller steps. Children learn to think ahead, plan their solution and check the program before running it.

Debugging skills

Learn that programs don't always work the first time. Children develop problem-solving by spotting errors and fixing them through trial and error.

Rising difficulty

Thoughtful levels that build skills step by step. Each level introduces something new and builds on the familiar. Challenging but never overwhelming.

Ideal from age 4

Designed for children from age 4. No prior knowledge needed. Suitable for complete beginners. Teaches real foundations of computational thinking.
